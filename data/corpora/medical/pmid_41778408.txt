TI  - Acute interleukin-1beta antagonism decreases systemic inflammation, increases GLP-1 
      release, and modulates insulin secretion in individuals with prediabetes.
PG  - E500-E509
LID - 10.1152/ajpendo.00527.2025 [doi]
AB  - Metabolic stress in obesity and diabetes activates innate immunity. Chronic IL-1beta 
      antagonism improves insulin secretion in type 2 diabetes. However, it is unclear 
      how rapidly this effect occurs, whether it is also present in individuals with 
      prediabetes, and if IL-1beta influences GLP-1 secretion. Therefore, we acutely 
      antagonized IL-1beta in patients with prediabetes overnight. Two injections of 
      anakinra significantly reduced total leucocyte count (P < 0.001), neutrophils (P 
      < 0.001), monocytes (P = 0.006), and CRP (P = 0.030) compared with placebo. 
      Lymphocytes were slightly elevated (P = 0.045). A mixed meal tolerance tests 
      showed a trend toward increased early insulin (P = 0.11) and GLP-1 responses (P = 
      0.055), with GLP-1 (P = 0.020) and glucagon (P = 0.003) levels being 
      significantly higher at 120 min under anakinra. Cytokine analysis showed elevated 
      baseline concentrations of IL-1beta, IL-6, and IL-1Ra under anakinra (all P < 
      0.001), with IL-1beta (P < 0.001) and IL-18BP (P = 0.048) decreased, and IL-6 
      increased (P = 0.059) after 60 min. Therefore, an acute injection of anakinra 
      significantly reduces CRP and leucocyte counts in individuals with prediabetes, 
      indicating effective innate immune modulation even after immediate treatment. 
      Despite seeing no significant improvements in insulin secretion or glucose 
      metabolism, we observed a trend toward earlier insulin secretion, along with 
      increased release of IL-6 and GLP-1. We conclude that acute IL-1 antagonism 
      dampens systemic inflammation in prediabetes, with the potential to improve 
      insulin secretion via IL-6-induced GLP-1.NEW & NOTEWORTHY Acute IL-1 blockade 
      with anakinra markedly reduced CRP and leukocyte counts within 12 h, 
      demonstrating rapid anti-inflammatory efficacy. Anakinra induced a trend toward 
      earlier insulin secretion and significantly increased postprandial IL-6 and GLP-1 
      levels. The study demonstrates that even short-term IL-1 blockade can modulate 
      both immune and incretin responses in prediabetes. Early IL-1beta antagonism may 
      represent a preventive, anti-inflammatory approach to preserve GLP-1 secretion 
      and beta-cell function in individuals with prediabetes.
